Cholangiocarcinoma

Cholangiocarcinomas can present as mass-forming lesions with distinctive characteristics:

  • Typically solitary but may be multifocal.
  • Continuous rim enhancement on arterial phase images.
  • Fibrotic progressive enhancement on delayed images.
  • Uniform appearance in both pathology and gross specimen.
  • Desmoplastic tumors with a targatoid appearance and hepatic retraction.
  • Reverse washout sign, indicating progressive enhancement with less contrast in the periphery.

These features, including the targatoid appearance and specific enhancement patterns, are crucial for making a precise diagnosis of cholangiocarcinoma. The information underscores the importance of thorough imaging analysis, encompassing CT and MR findings.
